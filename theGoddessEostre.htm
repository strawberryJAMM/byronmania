As she appeared on an egg

Easter Morning 2005

 

My family decorates eggs every Saturday evening before Easter Sunday.  The adults have used traditional European methods of wax resist and the children dye, paint and draw.

 

Just after midnight on March 27th, 2005 – Easter morning – an egg came out of the dye bath with this amazing natural image.  It has not been altered.  Click on the links to view three full size views of the egg.

 

Eostre is the Anglo-Saxon goddess of fertility, birth and the re-birth of all life in springtime.  Her companion is a hare or rabbit.  Her name was given to the Christian spring festival  - Easter. 

 

Blessed be!
